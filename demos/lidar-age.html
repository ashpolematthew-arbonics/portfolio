🌲 Tree age from LiDAR → growth database real data

Real individual-tree crowns detected from Estonia's national airborne laser scanning (ALS), aged with a model trained on terrestrial-LiDAR reference trees. This is one 1 km tile of a 1.2-million-tree national inventory. The headline: a single, subtle crown-area calibration decides whether this stand looks middle-aged or ancient — and that flows straight into the carbon curve. Toggle it and watch.

How this works — data & method (from ALS_GROWTH_DB_METHODOLOGY.md)

Real data. Estonia's Land Board distributes national ALS as 1 km tiles (EPSG:3301) with multispectral returns. This demo shows tile of a national run of 1.2 million detected crowns, exported to a static file the browser reads directly.

Detection. Point cloud → 0.5 m Canopy Height Model → variable-window local maxima (Popescu & Wynne 2004) → marker-controlled watershed (Meyer & Beucher 1990) for crown polygons. A per-return NDVI live/dead filter drops ~10.6% of geometric "crowns" that are roofs or standing dead wood.

The crown-area correction. A watershed partitions the canopy, so its crown area exceeds the true crown projection that terrestrial LiDAR (TLS) measures — by a median factor of 2.3×. Since crown area is an age-model feature and the model extrapolates upward on big crowns, the raw inventory has an artefactual old tail. Rescaling area by s = 0.43 ≈ 12.8/29.5 (median-anchored to the TLS reference) collapses it. In this tile that moves ≥80 yr crowns from 44% to 10% and the median age from 57 to 36 yr — exactly the toggle above.

Age & honest uncertainty. Age comes from a gradient-boosted model trained on the FORage TLS dataset (height, crown diameter/area, species). Because height saturates with age (Eichhorn's rule), old ages are imprecise — so each estimate carries a validation-derived reliability envelope banded by predicted age (RMSE 6.6 yr at 0–20; 80 yr at ≥80), and ≥80 yr trees are treated as right-censored floors, not point ages. The lower panel shows height saturating and the reliability bands.

Into the growth database. Species come from zero-shot CLIP on the RGB orthophoto; site index (SI₅₀) and Bonitet are derived, and a DBH is synthesised via the inverse Näslund height–DBH model so these height-only records fold (down-weighted, provenance-tagged) into the Chapman–Richards DBH–age growth curves that drive ex-ante carbon.

Pipeline export: pipeline/build_als_inventory.py; full methodology in the ex_ante_europe repo.
