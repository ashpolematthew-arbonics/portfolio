🛰️ Drone site eligibility assessment real data

A real UAV orthomosaic of a Sudano-Sahelian agroforestry site in Senegal (via OpenAerialMap, CC-BY). I classify the drone imagery into land cover from its visible bands, then screen it for afforestation eligibility — excluding existing tree canopy (the additionality / forest test), applying a canopy setback and a minimum block size. Drag the controls and the eligible area re-screens live over the real image.

How this works — data & method

Real drone data. The basemap is an actual UAV orthomosaic — — hosted on OpenAerialMap (, flown ). The COG is read and warped to WGS-84 in a Python pipeline; the browser streams the full-resolution tiles directly.

Classification. With only visible RGB bands (no near-infrared), land cover is derived from the Excess-Green index and local texture: tree canopy is vegetation that is both darker and rougher than the surrounding crop/grass matrix, giving canopy cover across the site — the rest is herbaceous cropland/fallow with small bare and water patches. It's an honest visible-band estimate, not a NIR/LiDAR product.

Eligibility. Plantable land is open cover (herbaceous + optionally bare). A pixel is excluded if it already carries tree canopy (additionality / the 10-year forest test), sits within the canopy setback of existing trees (using a distance-to-canopy surface), or falls in a plantable block below the minimum size (a connected-components filter). Eligible area drives a first-order planting and ex-ante carbon estimate.
