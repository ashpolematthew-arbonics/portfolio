🗺️ Drone participatory mapping

Participatory mapping puts the pen in the community's hand: landholders trace their own boundaries over high-resolution drone orthoimagery, building a shared, geo-referenced record of land tenure and use. Use the draw tools on the left of the map to digitise parcels — area, perimeter and a running land-cover summary update as you go, and you can export the whole thing as GeoJSON.

How this works — the method behind the demo

The basemap is high-resolution imagery standing in for a drone orthomosaic — a centimetre-scale, geo-referenced photo built by stitching hundreds of overlapping UAV images via structure-from-motion.

Polygons are digitised in the browser and areas computed with the spherical excess formula on the WGS-84 ellipsoid (the same maths Leaflet/Turf use), giving true ground area regardless of latitude. Each parcel is tagged with a land-cover class and serialised to GeoJSON — the interchange format that feeds straight into QGIS, PostGIS or a carbon-project MRV database.

In the field this runs offline on a tablet, letting communities verify and correct machine land-cover classifications against what they know on the ground.
