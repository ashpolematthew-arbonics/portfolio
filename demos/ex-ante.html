📈 Ex-ante carbon calculation

Before a single credit is issued, a project must forecast how much carbon it will sequester. This tool fits a Chapman–Richards growth curve to species- and region-specific biomass, projects it forward, and converts it to issuable tCO₂e after applying a non-permanence buffer. Drag the controls — everything recomputes in your browser.

How this works — the method behind the demo

Biomass accumulation is modelled with the Chapman–Richards function, the workhorse of forest growth modelling:

B(t) = Bmax · (1 − e−k·t)p

where B_max is the asymptotic biomass (tCO₂e/ha) set by species × site, k governs growth rate and p the sigmoidal shape. Annual carbon increment is the year-on-year difference, multiplied by project area and stocking.

The uncertainty band comes from a Monte-Carlo run (250 draws) that perturbs k and p within calibration bounds, then takes the P5–P95 envelope — mirroring how ex-ante uncertainty is propagated in practice.

Net issuable credits apply a non-permanence buffer withholding (à la Verra AFOLU): net = gross × (1 − buffer). In production this also nets out baseline and leakage; those are held at zero here for clarity.
