🛰️ Multispectral drone restoration planning real data

My own DJI Mavic 3 Multispectral flights (G / R / red-edge / NIR + RGB) over degraded savanna near Bolgatanga, northern Ghana. Each frame is band-registered, turned into real NDVI & NDRE, classified, and every major tree crown delineated — then each patch of ground is matched to the right restoration intervention: Agroforestry on cultivated land, Reforestation on bare degraded ground, and Assisted Natural Regeneration where shrubs are already coming back. Switch scenes and layers; it all runs in your browser.

How this works — data & method (with literature)

Sensor. DJI Mavic 3M, ~5.7 cm GSD. The four MS lenses (G, R, RE, NIR) have parallax offsets, co-registered to red by high-pass phase cross-correlation; the wider-FOV 20 MP RGB is fitted to the MS grid by ORB features + RANSAC similarity.

Indices. NDVI (Rouse 1974) and red-edge NDRE (Gitelson & Merzlyak 1994). Tree crowns: smoothed-NDVI local maxima (Popescu & Wynne 2004) + marker-controlled watershed (Ke & Quackenbush 2011).

Matching land to intervention. Rather than a single "eligible" mask, each patch is matched to the restoration approach that fits its condition — the way NbS programmes actually plan:

  • Agroforestry — open ground inside a delineated field parcel (a large, low-vegetation Felzenszwalb segment with definable borders). Trees are integrated into the farming system; cultivation is a positive, not an exclusion.
  • Reforestation — bare, minimally-vegetated degraded ground outside field parcels. Direct tree planting.
  • Assisted Natural Regeneration (ANR) — shrub / woody regrowth with few mature trees. Protect and manage what is already returning.
  • Existing canopy — delineated mature crowns are conserved, not planted (the forest test).

Blocks, not pixels. The per-pixel intervention map is coarsened, morphologically cleaned and vectorised into contiguous polygons, so the output is a set of manageable restoration units rather than salt-and-pepper pixels — filter them by minimum block size. Each intervention carries an indicative sequestration rate for a first-order ex-ante estimate.

Limitations — read before trusting any of this. This is unsupervised classification of a single, un-calibrated, dry-season UAV frame, and that is a genuinely hard, error-prone setting:

  • High resolution hurts as much as it helps. At ~6 cm, one tree is thousands of mixed, shadowed, sunlit pixels; spectral classes overlap heavily and unsupervised methods over-segment. Reported class fractions and crown counts can shift materially with threshold choices — they are indicative, not measured.
  • No training data or calibration. No reflectance panel (indices are relative DN ratios), no field plots, no labels — so classes are inferred from geometry and spectra alone and cannot be accuracy-assessed here. A defensible product needs ground-truth points and a supervised or manually-audited classifier.
  • Dry-season ambiguity. Ploughed cropland and degraded bare soil are both bare and near-identical spectrally; the field-parcel and tillage cues are proxies, not proof of active cultivation. Multi-season NDVI (crop phenology) is needed to confirm land use.
  • Ecology is not in the pixels. Whether a patch should be reforested, farmed with trees or left to regenerate depends on tenure, species suitability, soil, hydrology, grazing and community priorities — knowledge that only local expertise and field inspection provide. This tool narrows and structures where to look; it does not replace a walk-through with the land users.
  • Frames are single nadir captures (no orthomosaic/PPK here), and altitude wasn't logged, so area is estimated at ~100 m AGL.
