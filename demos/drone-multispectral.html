🛰️ Multispectral drone land-cover & crown analysis real data

My own DJI Mavic 3 Multispectral capture (green / red / red-edge / near-infrared + RGB) over degraded savanna near Bolgatanga, northern Ghana — Great Green Wall country. The four spectral bands are registered, turned into real NDVI & NDRE, classified into woody vegetation vs open ground, and every major tree crown is delineated — then screened for afforestation suitability. Switch layers and drag the rules; it all runs in your browser.

How this works — data & method (with literature)

Sensor. DJI Mavic 3M, ~5.7 cm ground sampling. The four multispectral lenses (G, R, RE, NIR) have parallax offsets, so each band is co-registered to red by high-pass phase cross-correlation, and the 20 MP RGB (wider FOV) is fitted to the MS grid with an ORB feature + RANSAC similarity transform.

Indices. NDVI = (NIR−R)/(NIR+R) (Rouse 1974) and the red-edge NDRE = (NIR−RE)/(NIR+RE) (Gitelson & Merzlyak 1994), which tracks canopy chlorophyll and helps separate woody canopy from dry herbaceous cover.

Tree-crown delineation. Smoothed-NDVI local maxima with a height-scaled window (Popescu & Wynne 2004) seed a marker-controlled watershed (Meyer & Beucher 1990; Ke & Quackenbush 2011); crowns are filtered by size, red-edge greenness and prominence to keep major trees (mean crown ~ m).

Land cover. A robust three-class map — tree crown / shrub-woody / open ground — from the NDVI canopy surface plus the crown segmentation.

Suitability. Existing woody canopy is excluded (additionality / the forest test); a canopy setback (distance-to-canopy surface) and a minimum block (connected components) refine the open ground into a plantable area, driving a first-order stem & ex-ante estimate.

Honest limitations. This is a single dry-season frame: active cropland and degraded bare soil are both spectrally bare, so they can't be reliably split here (a "tillage" texture layer flags likely-ploughed ground, but separating cultivation needs multi-season NDVI). The RGB has no radiometric calibration panel, so indices are relative. Flight altitude wasn't logged, so area is estimated at ~100 m AGL.
