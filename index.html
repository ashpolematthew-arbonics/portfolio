Biomass-map disagreement audit real data

Two published satellite biomass maps — Spawn 2010 and NASA GEDI L4B — over a real dryland window in northern Ghana. They agree on the regional total to 2% yet disagree ~48% pixel-by-pixel. Swipe the maps and see what that does to a project's carbon baseline.

About

I'm a nature-based solutions specialist, ecological data scientist and forester working across the full measurement, reporting and verification (MRV) chain of voluntary carbon projects — from eligible-land analysis and baseline setting to ex-ante modelling, field inventory and verification.

I'm currently a Forest Carbon Analyst at Arbonics, developing proprietary ex-ante forest growth models for projects in the Baltics and leading MRV and verification processes. Before that, as Nature-based Solutions & Carbon Manager at Tree Aid, I led carbon quantification across the dryland projects of West Africa and delivered the organisation's first VM0047-registered project (VCS ID 5085) — running eligible-land and change-detection analyses, ex-ante projections from forest inventory, and introducing drone monitoring with semantic-segmentation crown delineation, while closing technical-partner investment deals worth over $20m.

Earlier roles span DAERA (environmental impact assessment), Wild Asia (oil-palm GHG accounting) and consulting for the Landscapes & Livelihoods Group across Kenya, Uganda and Sierra Leone. I hold an MSc in Ecological Economics (University of Leeds) and a BSc in Environmental Science (University of Birmingham), and co-authored a World Economic Forum & Tree Aid report on Great Green Wall carbon-market potential.

The demos here are self-contained reconstructions of this work using synthetic data — nothing exposes client or proprietary information, but the methods, formulas and structure are the real thing.
